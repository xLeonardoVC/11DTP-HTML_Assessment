Blocking

Blocking in volleyball is a crucial defensive skill that can significantly impact the game. Here are essential techniques and tips to help beginners master blocking.

  • Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and knees slightly bent. This stance allows for quick movement and jumping. Keep your shoulders and hips square to the net to facilitate a straight jump.
  • Jump just after the attacker leaves the ground. This timing ensures you are at your peak when they hit the ball, maximizing your chances of a successful block.
  • Keep your arms high with elbows at shoulder height and palms facing the net. This position allows for a quicker reaction and better coverage of the net.
  • Use a deep squat to generate power for your jump. As you jump, extend your arms upward to reach over the net, pressing your hands down to block the ball effectively.
  • Keep your eyes on the ball and the hitter. Anticipating their move can help you position yourself better for the block.
